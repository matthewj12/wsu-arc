Introducing... ARC: The WSU Animal Rights Club!

Current meeting times:
Wed 4:30 - 5:30 PM (on even-numbered Wed/Fri)

The first normal meeting will be held on Wed, Sep 14.



Are you an animal lover? Does it pain you to see your fellow creatures suffer? Do you long for a world where all sentient beings are afforded the basic rights they deserve?

If so, ARC may very well be your next favorite club! As a brand-new organization, ARC offers the unique opportunity to help catalyze the shift within our community and society toward a more comprehensive, compassionate understanding of nonhuman animals as the conscious agents they are. Or perhaps you're simply interested in meeting some fellow animal lovers/animal rights advocates. Either way, ARC will almost certainly have something to offer both for the seasoned activist and open-minded inquirer alike.

Fill out the survey for available meeting times here. Contact matthew.jilk@go.winona.edu or wsuAnimalRightsClub@gmail.com for any questions, comments, or concerns (expect all further communications to be from the latter).

Potential club activities include:

  • discussing animal-related philosophy
  • open discussion, personal anecdotes, getting to know each other, etc.
  • public activism/awareness-raising
  • watching educational content regarding animal sentience, welfare, and policy (or lack thereof)
  • miscellaneous activities (it wouldn't be a real club without the occasional individualized study hall)

Note: this list is by no means binding or exhaustive; seeing as ARC is in its infancy, we're always open to new ideas and suggestions. Nothing has been written in stone (yet!).

A preliminary meetup (the first official ARC event!) will be held this coming Friday, Sep 9, at 5 PM in Gildemeister 161 to point prospective members toward the relevant resources, bounce ideas off each other, and -- most importantly -- map some names to faces! The event will be accessible remotely through Zoom via the link above.

Real talk: As it stands now, ARC is a cohort of three eleven. That's seven negative 1 short of the required ten to become an officially registered WSU organization, which would allow us to secure funding, consistent meeting locations, campus advertisement, and official listing as a WSU student organization in the winona.edu club directory. If you're genuinely captivated, even mildly, by what I just described, please don't neglect to contact me and/or attend the aforementioned preliminary meeting so we can formalize our group before the Sep 15 deadline. If you're interested in joining, do it sooner rather than later!
